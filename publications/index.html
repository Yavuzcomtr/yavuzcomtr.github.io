Are public transport information systems equally effective? Insights from local and migrant passengers
Abdulkadir Özden, Hakan Kocaman, Yavuz Halimergün
4th International Civil Engineering and Architecture Conference (ICEARC), Trabzon, Türkiye, 2025, pp. 2614–2621.
Abstract: This paper examines whether public transport information systems generate similar levels of benefit for local and migrant passengers. The study discusses differences in access, interpretation, and usability of travel information and highlights implications for equitable public transport planning.

Deprem Riskine Karşı Raylı Sistem Altyapılarının CBS ile Analizi: Türkiye Örneği
Hakan Kocaman, Süleyman Nurullah Adahi Şahin, Yavuz Halimergün, Abdulkadir Özden
7th International Symposium on Natural Hazards and Disaster Management, 2025.
Abstract: This study evaluates rail system infrastructures in Türkiye from the perspective of earthquake risk using GIS-based analysis methods. The paper focuses on the spatial vulnerability of critical transport infrastructure and underlines the importance of resilience-oriented transport planning.

Will new tram lines enhance the connectivity and walkability? A case study of Sakarya
Yavuz Halimergün, Abdulkadir Özden, Süleyman Nurullah Adahi Şahin, Hakan Kocaman
Sustainable Social Development, 2024, 2(5), Article ID 2886.
Abstract: This study evaluates the potential effects of newly proposed tram lines on urban connectivity and accessibility in Sakarya. Using a GIS-based analytical framework, the paper investigates how rail system investments may improve public transport integration and strengthen sustainable urban mobility.
DOI PDF

Walking Towards Sustainability: Optimizing Public Transit through the Walkable Cities by Time-Oriented Model
Süleyman Nurullah Adahi Şahin, Yavuz Halimergün, Abdulkadir Özden
60th ISOCARP World Planning Congress, Siena, Italy, 2024.
Abstract: This study discusses the relationship between walkability and public transport efficiency through a time-oriented urban accessibility perspective. The paper emphasizes that strengthening pedestrian access to transit systems can improve both public transport use and sustainable urban form.
