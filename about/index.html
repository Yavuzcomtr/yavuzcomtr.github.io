About

I am a civil engineer specializing in transport planning and public transportation systems, with a strong focus on GIS-based spatial analysis and sustainable urban mobility. My academic and professional work centers on understanding and improving the integration of different transport modes, particularly rail and bus systems, in rapidly developing urban environments.

My research primarily explores how public transport networks can be optimized through data-driven and spatially explicit approaches. I have worked extensively on tram-bus integration, accessibility analysis, and the evaluation of transport systems based on environmental and economic performance indicators. By combining Geographic Information Systems (GIS) with transport planning methodologies, I aim to develop practical and scalable solutions for more efficient and sustainable mobility systems.

In addition to network integration, I am particularly interested in walkability and accessibility in urban transport systems. My work examines how pedestrian access to transit infrastructure influences overall system performance and user behavior, contributing to the development of more inclusive and sustainable cities.

Professionally, I am involved in transport planning practices within a municipal context, where I contribute to real-world applications of transport policies, network design, and mobility strategies. This practical experience allows me to bridge the gap between academic research and implementation, ensuring that my work remains both theoretically grounded and operationally relevant.

My current research direction focuses on developing integrated, GIS-based analytical frameworks for evaluating and optimizing public transport systems. I aim to contribute to the advancement of sustainable urban mobility by supporting data-driven decision-making processes in transport planning and policy development.
